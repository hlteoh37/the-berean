Today's Devotional

John 1:19–28 — The Voice

Jerusalem sends a delegation: Who are you? John's answer is a triple negation — not the Christ, not Elijah, not the Prophet (vv. 20–21). Every title is refused. Then one claim: φωνὴ βοῶντος (phōnē boōntos, "a voice crying") — Isaiah 40:3. He is not the message; he is only the medium. Verse 27 sharpens it: "the one coming after me, whose sandal strap I am not ἄξιος (axios, worthy) to untie." Untying sandals was slave's work. John will not claim even that dignity for himself.

The greatest prophetic voice of his era spent it pointing entirely away from himself. True witness erases the witness.

Featured Answers

What does John 3:16 mean?

"For God so loved the world" — but οὕτως (houtōs) means "in this way," not merely "so much." The emphasis is on how God loved: by giving his μονογενής (monogenēs), his one and only Son. The gift is the costliest imaginable. Faith (πιστεύων, present participle — ongoing trust) is the one human act: receiving what God has given.

What is repentance?

Repentance is μετάνοια (metanoia) — a change of mind that produces a change of direction. It is not mere remorse (Judas was remorseful, not repentant). The Hebrew שׁוּב (shuv) adds the image of turning: away from sin, back toward God. Repentance and faith are two sides of one conversion — you cannot truly trust Christ while clinging to sin. Luther was right in Thesis 1: "the entire life of believers" is one of repentance.
