Walking through the Gospel of John

Currently in: The Gospel of John
March 19, 2026 2 min read

John 1:19-28 — The Voice

Jerusalem sends a delegation: Who are you? John's answer is a triple negation — not the Christ, not Elijah, not the Prophet (vv. 20-21). Every title is refused. Then one claim: φωνὴ βοῶντος (phōnē boōntos, "a voice crying") — Isaiah 40:3. He is not the message; he is only the medium. Verse 27 sharpens it further: "the one coming after me, whose sandal strap I am not ἄξιος (axios, worthy) to untie." Untying sandals was slave's work. John will not claim even that dignity for himself.

The greatest prophetic voice of his era spent it pointing entirely away from himself. True witness erases the witness.

John 1:14-18 — The Tabernacling Word

The Logos ἐσκήνωσεν (eskēnōsen) — "tabernacled" among us, pitching his tent in human flesh. The word echoes the Sinai tabernacle where God's glory descended (Exod. 40). John's χάριτος καὶ ἀληθείας (charitos kai alētheias, "grace and truth") deliberately mirrors Exodus 34:6 — the very character God proclaimed to Moses now becomes visible in a person. Verse 18 is stunning: the Son ἐξηγήσατο (exēgēsato) the Father — the verb from which we get "exegesis." Jesus is the divine exegete, the final word on who God is, speaking from the Father's κόλπον (kolpon, "bosom") — intimacy become revelation.

No one has ever seen God — but his Son, who dwells in the Father's heart, has made him fully known. Christ is God's own self-interpretation.

John 1:6-13 — The Witness and the Rejected Light

John the Baptist enters as ἄνθρωπος ἀπεσταλμένος (anthrōpos apestalmenos), "a man sent" — explicitly not the Light, but a witness to it. The true Light (τὸ φῶς τὸ ἀληθινόν, v. 9) came into the world he made, yet "the world did not know him" — οὐκ ἔγνω, a sorrowful aorist verdict. Even his own people (οἱ ἴδιοι, hoi idioi, Israel) refused him. But to those who receive him, God grants ἐξουσία (exousia, "right/authority") to become children of God — born not of blood nor human will, but of God alone.

Rejection is the world's verdict on its Maker. But receiving him by faith crosses you from darkness into sonship.

John 1:1-5 — The Word and the Light

John opens with a deliberate echo of Genesis: "Ἐν ἀρχῇ" (en archē, "In the beginning"). The Word (λόγος, logos) was not created — the imperfect ἦν ("was") indicates eternal pre-existence, not origin. Three declarations establish his identity: present at creation, distinct from the Father, yet himself fully God (θεός without the article, stressing divine nature). Verse 3 leaves nothing outside his creative act. Then life enters the frame: ζωή (zōē) shines as the light of humanity, and the darkness has not overcome it — κατέλαβεν means both "comprehended" and "conquered." Both fail against the Word.

All of creation, all of life, all of light flows from the eternal Word. The darkness cannot win — it never has.

Now these Jews were more noble than those in Thessalonica; they received the word with all eagerness, examining the Scriptures daily to see if these things were so. — Acts 17:11 (ESV)
